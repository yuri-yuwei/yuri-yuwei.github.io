2024年8月4–9日 · 美国加利福尼亚州长滩

Wei, Y., Chen, H., & Qiu, T. (2024). Imaging spectroscopy captures temperature-dependent plant biodiversity across the United States. Ecological Society of America Annual Meeting.

Abstract
摘要

Biodiversity provides many ecosystem services and functions that human well-being is reliant on. Under climate change and disturbances caused by human activities, the loss of global biodiversity has become a growing concern. The integration of remote sensing technology with ecological sampling, particularly linking spectral diversity derived from hyperspectral imagery to ground biodiversity, has emerged as a technical frontier in assessing terrestrial plant diversity. Phylogenetic diversity, a measure of variations in phylogenetic aspects of a plant community, is directly related to the ecosystem functions provided by plants. However, the dynamics of phylogenetic diversity at large scales remain poorly understood. To fill these knowledge gaps, we aim to understand: (1) which dimensions of plant biodiversity, such as taxonomic and phylogenetic diversity, can be more effectively assessed by the Spectral Diversity–Biodiversity relationship; and (2) which environmental factors influence the strength of SDB relationships across broad biogeographic regions. We synthesized ground sampling data from 10,172 individual crown measurements and 74,093 plant percentage cover records within the National Ecological Observatory Network to estimate plant biodiversity across 38 NEON sites. Hyperspectral imagery was derived from the NEON Airborne Observation Platform, with soil pixels removed using adaptive NDVI thresholding masks.

生物多样性提供了人类福祉所依赖的多种生态系统服务与功能。在气候变化和人类活动扰动背景下，全球生物多样性下降已成为日益严峻的生态问题。将遥感技术与生态调查相结合，尤其是将高光谱影像衍生的光谱多样性与地面生物多样性联系起来，已成为陆地植物多样性评估的重要技术前沿。系统发育多样性用于衡量植物群落在系统发育维度上的差异，并与植物提供的生态系统功能密切相关。然而，大尺度系统发育多样性的空间格局与动态仍缺乏充分认识。为弥补这一知识缺口，本研究关注两个问题：（1）哪些植物多样性维度，例如分类多样性和系统发育多样性，能够更有效地通过光谱多样性—生物多样性关系进行评估；（2）哪些环境因子会影响不同生物地理区域中 SDB 关系的强度。研究综合了 NEON 观测网络中 10,172 条单木冠层测量记录和 74,093 条植物百分盖度记录，在 38 个 NEON 站点估算植物多样性。高光谱影像来自 NEON Airborne Observation Platform，并通过自适应 NDVI 阈值掩膜去除土壤像元。

Wei, Y., Chen, H., Kovach, K. R., Townsend, P. A., & Qiu, T. (2024). Functional diversity as a predictor of ecosystem functions: Evidence from imaging spectroscopy. AGU Fall Meeting.

Abstract
摘要

Plant functional diversity is a key indicator of various ecosystem functions, including primary production, plant health, water cycles, decomposition, and ecosystem stability. Higher functional diversity has been documented to facilitate resource and niche partitioning, leading to enhanced ecosystem functions such as accelerated carbon cycles and more effective nutrient transition within the food web. However, studies on the connection between functional diversity and ecosystem functions were usually restricted to small scales due to the high costs and low efficiencies of data collection. Leveraging evidence from imaging spectroscopy, this study aims to understand how the functional diversity–ecosystem function relationship varies across different biomes in North America.

植物功能多样性是表征多种生态系统功能的重要指标，包括初级生产力、植物健康状况、水循环、分解过程和生态系统稳定性。较高的功能多样性通常能够促进资源利用和生态位分化，从而增强生态系统功能，例如加速碳循环过程，并提高食物网中养分转移的效率。然而，由于野外数据采集成本高、效率低，关于功能多样性与生态系统功能关系的研究通常局限于较小空间尺度。本研究利用成像光谱提供的证据，评估北美不同生物群系中功能多样性—生态系统功能关系的变化。
